    Recorded Kind Reason / Arch Miner Height

    Bounded ring buffer of the most recent rejection records plus per-field rune-truncation counters, the full persistence lifecycle (errors / compactions / on-disk record count / hard-cap drops), and the per-miner rate-limit drop counter. Truncation is applied before records reach the ring, so high truncated counts indicate possible hostile input — not data loss in the buffer itself. A sustained compactions rate means a miner is filling the ring faster than the soft-cap can absorb; non-zero hard-cap drops means a flood is outrunning even the rewrite cycle and the on-disk durability ceiling has refused records (in-memory ring is unaffected); non-zero rate-limit drops means a single miner has exhausted their per-miner token bucket and records from that source are being shed at ring entry (neither ring nor persister touched). Use the kind / window filters above to narrow the table, and pause polling while reading. See QSD_attest_rejection_field_*, QSD_attest_rejection_persist_errors_total, QSD_attest_rejection_persist_compactions_total, QSD_attest_rejection_persist_records_on_disk, QSD_attest_rejection_persist_hardcap_drops_total, and QSD_attest_rejection_per_miner_rate_limited_total for Prometheus equivalents.

      Bounded in-memory store of the most recent v2-mining slash receipts (applied + rejected outcomes), plus a snapshot of the QSD_slash_* counters. The applied cell tallies successful slashes by EvidenceKind; drained dust shows the cumulative stake forfeited (CELL = dust ÷ 1e9); reward / burn splits the drained total into slasher reward vs. burned residue; rejected is per-reason; auto-revoked tallies post-slash record revocations from SlashApplier.RevokeIfUnderBonded. A sustained non-zero rejected rate signals a buggy slasher tool or an upstream probe; a drained dust spike of multiple CELL in a few minutes indicates a coordinated cheat ring or a verifier regression — see the SLASHING_INCIDENT.md runbook (linked from every QSDMining* alert's runbook_url) for the triage flow.
